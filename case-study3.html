Zhiwa CTG: Cardiotocography Monitoring System for Maternal Healthcare

AI-assisted fetal heart rate & contraction monitoring system for clinical decision support

Product Overview

Zhiwa CTG is a digital cardiotocography (CTG) monitoring system designed to visualize and analyze fetal heart rate (FHR) and uterine contraction patterns during pregnancy and labor. The system provides healthcare professionals with a clear, real-time graphical representation of CTG signals to support maternal and fetal health assessment.

The primary objective of this project is to improve the accessibility and efficiency of fetal monitoring by digitizing CTG data visualization and reducing reliance on traditional paper-based CTG strips. In many clinical environments, CTG interpretation can be time-consuming and prone to manual reading errors.

Zhiwa CTG addresses this by providing a structured digital interface that displays real-time or recorded CTG waveforms, enabling easier interpretation of fetal conditions. The system also supports basic anomaly detection concepts to highlight abnormal patterns such as fetal distress indicators.

The system was designed with simplicity and clinical usability in mind, ensuring that healthcare workers can quickly interpret readings without unnecessary complexity. It focuses on clear visualization, smooth data flow, and fast rendering of CTG signals.

Problem Statement

In many healthcare settings, fetal monitoring using cardiotocography is still heavily dependent on manual interpretation of printed CTG strips. This creates challenges such as delayed analysis, interpretation inconsistencies, and difficulty in long-term record tracking.

Healthcare professionals often need to make quick decisions during labor, and manual CTG reading can increase cognitive load and risk of oversight, especially during high-pressure situations.

Additionally, there is limited adoption of simple, localized digital CTG visualization systems in Bhutanese healthcare environments. Most existing solutions are either expensive, complex, or not adapted to local clinical workflows.

Zhiwa CTG aims to solve this by offering a lightweight, digital-first CTG monitoring interface that improves readability, accessibility, and data interpretation efficiency.

Target Users

The primary users of Zhiwa CTG are doctors, nurses, and healthcare professionals involved in maternal care and labor monitoring.

The system is also useful for medical trainees and hospital staff who need simplified visualization of CTG data for learning and interpretation purposes.

It is designed to reduce complexity in CTG interpretation and provide a clean, accessible interface for quick clinical decision-making.

Key Features

  • Real-time fetal heart rate (FHR) and contraction monitoring visualization.
  • Dual-graph CTG layout (FHR + uterine contractions).
  • Time-series data rendering for continuous monitoring.
  • Simple, clinical-friendly dashboard interface.
  • Support for recorded CTG playback for review and analysis.
  • Fast rendering for near real-time decision support.

Data Processing & System

The system processes time-series CTG data representing fetal heart rate and uterine contraction signals. These signals are structured and visualized using charting libraries for real-time display.

Basic signal smoothing techniques are applied to reduce noise and improve readability of CTG waveforms.

Usability Study & Testing

Testing focused on usability in clinical environments, especially readability of CTG graphs and speed of interpretation.

Findings

  • Users preferred simplified dual-graph layout.
  • Clear labeling improved interpretation speed.
  • Real-time updates improved clinical responsiveness.

Solution & Implementation

Zhiwa CTG was implemented as a web-based visualization system that processes CTG time-series data and displays it in a structured graphical format.

The system prioritizes clarity, speed, and accessibility for healthcare professionals working in time-critical environments.

Challenges Faced
